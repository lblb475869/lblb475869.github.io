创始人 / Founder

中文版

李霖豪 独立学者，现代东方医学（Modern Oriental Medicine, MOM）创始人。

他长期专注于医学体系的重构与原创理论的建立，先后提出炁能–生液模型、分形全息方法论、全息普适原理、泛全息身体映射模型、全局矩阵模型、对称映射平衡模型等多个原创体系；并在此基础上，发展出全息脉诊、全息诊断规程、全息映射疗法、分形泛全息疗法等方法。相关原创内容已在 Zenodo、Figshare 等国际平台完成公开存证与时间戳确权。

代表性著作包括《双手脉诊》（线装书局，2023）、《唐本伤寒论解读》、《脉诊双法不修心》等，以及多篇原创声明与方法学论文。他以语言逻辑、分形全息与系统映射为核心方法，结合分形、矩阵、对称等数学工具与复杂系统、信息映射等跨学科视角，旨在更精确地描述人体失衡，建立可执行的判断流程，并设计可复现的干预路径。

在学术研究之外，他积极推动 MOM 的课程化与普及化。多门课程已上线，将复杂的原创体系转化为结构化、可学习、可操作、可复现的教学模块，使学习者能够在较短时间内掌握脉诊、针法、自然药物疗法等多种实用体系。

学术定位：独立学者，跨学科医学创新研究者。研究方向涵盖脉诊、针法、食疗与自然药物疗法、全息映射理论、对称与矩阵化的人体结构表达等，兼顾临床应用与国际学术沟通，持续推进现代东方医学的标准化、课程化与全球化。

English Version

Li Linhao — Independent scholar and Founder of Modern Oriental Medicine (MOM).

He focuses on reconstructing medical systems through original theoretical frameworks, including the Qi–Energy and Liquid-Generation Model (QEL), Fractal Holographic Methodology (FHM), Holographic Universal Principle (HUP), Pan-Holographic Body Mapping (PHBM), Global Matrix Model (GMM), and Symmetry Mapping Balance Model (SMBM), among others. Building on these systems, he has developed methods and applications such as Holographic Pulse Mapping, the Holographic Diagnostic Protocol, Holographic Mapping Therapy, and Fractal Pan-Holographic Therapy. These works have been publicly timestamped on international platforms such as Zenodo and Figshare with DOIs.

Representative works include Dual-Hand Pulse Diagnosis (Xianzhang Press, 2023), Tang-Version Shanghan Lun Interpretation, Pulse Diagnosis Double-Method Without Cultivating the Heart, and multiple declarations of originality and methodological papers. His approach integrates language logic, fractal holography, and systemic mapping with tools from mathematics (fractal, matrix, symmetry), complex systems, and information theory—aiming to describe imbalance precisely, codify executable diagnostic processes, and design reproducible interventions.

Beyond theory, he advances MOM through education and public dissemination. Several courses are now available, translating complex models into structured, learnable, and reproducible modules, enabling learners to rapidly acquire practical competencies in pulse diagnosis, needling methods, and natural therapeutics.

Academic positioning: Independent scholar and cross-disciplinary medical innovation researcher. His work spans pulse diagnosis, needling methods, dietary therapy and natural therapeutics, holographic mapping theory, and symmetry/matrix-based representations of the human body—serving both clinical application and international academic dialogue while continuously promoting the standardization, curriculum development, and globalization of MOM.
